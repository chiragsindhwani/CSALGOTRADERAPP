Strategy Overview

SPY Iron Condor 0DTE — Breakeven Iron Condor Method
Sell a same-day-expiring Iron Condor on SPY every trading day at 10:15 AM ET. The strategy profits from theta decay when SPY stays range-bound through the session. Risk is strictly defined — maximum loss is capped at the wing width minus credit received. With a tight 15% profit target and 45% stop loss, even stopped-out days remain net positive. Target: ~$200 per day with 30 contracts (~$4,560 buying power required).

📐 Payoff Diagram

MAX LOSS | MAX LOSS | ↓ PROFIT ZONE (keep full premium) ↓
──────────|Long Put|Short Put|────────────────|Short Call|Long Call|───
LP SP SC LC (SP-$2) (~15 delta) (~15 delta) (SC+$2)
Example: SPY @ $580 LP=$573 | SP=$575 | [profit zone] | SC=$585 | LC=$587 Net credit: ~$0.85–$1.20 per Iron Condor Max risk: $2.00 – credit = ~$0.80–$1.15 per share ($80–$115/contract)

🟢 Entry Rules

  • 1
    Time: 10:15 AM ET — after the opening volatility spike settles. Bid-ask spreads are tighter and direction is established.
  • 2
    Instrument: SPY 0DTE options (expire same day). Available Mon–Fri.
  • 3
    Short strikes: Sell call and put at ~15 delta (~1.5 standard deviations OTM). Calculated with Black-Scholes using VIX as implied volatility.
  • 4
    Wing width: Buy protection leg $2.00 away from each short strike. Maximum loss is capped at $2.00 − credit received.
  • 5
    Minimum credit: Only enter if total credit ≥ $0.40 ($40 per contract). Skip if credit is too low.

🚫 Skip / Avoid Days

⚠ High-Risk Days — Do NOT Trade
These days carry elevated gamma risk that can blow through stops before orders fill.
  • !
    FOMC announcement days — Fed rate decisions cause explosive moves in SPY within seconds. Skip the entire trading day.
  • !
    VIX above 30 — Extreme implied volatility widens bid-ask spreads to unprofitable levels. Wait for VIX to normalise.
  • !
    Major macro events before 10 AM ET — CPI, NFP, GDP releases that cause pre-market gaps >1% in SPY. Consider delaying entry to 10:30 AM or skipping.

🎯 Exit Rules

  • A
    Profit Target — 15% of Credit: Close the entire Iron Condor when the position has gained 15% of premium received.
    Example: collected $1.00 → close when buyback costs $0.85 → profit = $0.15/share ($15/contract).
  • B
    Stop Loss — 45% of Credit: Close a threatened spread when its cost to close reaches 45% of the total credit received.
    Example: collected $1.00 total → stop triggers at $0.45 cost on one side.
    Net outcome: still net positive — since 45% < 100% of credit, the overall position remains profitable even on a stop.
  • C
    Force Close — 3:45 PM ET: Close ALL open legs regardless of P&L. Never hold 0DTE options to expiration. Gamma risk becomes extreme in the final 15 minutes.

🛡️ Risk Management

  • R1
    Fixed Position Size — 30 Contracts: This account is dedicated to SPY Iron Condor only. Always trade exactly 30 contracts per day, sized to target ~$200 daily profit. Buying power required: ~$4,560 (max loss × 30 contracts).
  • R2
    One Trade Per Day: One Iron Condor position (30 contracts) only. Do not re-enter or add contracts intraday regardless of outcome.
  • R3
    Stop Loss is Still Profitable: With SL at 45% of credit and PT at 15%, the stop triggers before the full credit is consumed — meaning even stopped-out days generate a net gain. The opposite unchallenged side retains its remaining value.
  • R4
    Double Stop (both sides threatened simultaneously): Close all legs immediately. Max loss = (wing − credit) × 100 × 30 contracts = ~$4,560. This is the only scenario where a day ends with a loss.
  • R5
    Weekly Review: If a double-stop occurs, review VIX and SPY range before the next session. Consider skipping the next day if conditions are still extreme.

💰 Contract Size & Capital Requirements

This account is dedicated to SPY Iron Condor 0DTE only
No per-day budget cap is applied. The strategy always trades exactly 30 contracts as configured, sized to target ~$200 profit per day. Capital requirement is based purely on the broker margin for defined-risk spreads.
  • C1
    Contract Size: 1 SPY options contract controls 100 shares. All P&L is multiplied by 100 per contract.
    Example: $0.48 credit/share → $48 cash received per contract → $1,439 for 30 contracts.
  • C2
    Buying Power per Contract: Broker reserves the max loss of the spread as margin.
    Max loss = (Wing − credit) × 100 = ($2.00 − $0.48) × 100 = ~$152 per contract.
    30 contracts → ~$4,560 buying power required per trade.
  • C3
    Minimum Capital to Trade 30 Contracts: $10,000 (absolute floor).
    Comfortable buffer: $15,000 to absorb a double-stop day (~$4,560 max loss) and continue trading the following session without margin pressure.
  • C4
    Profit Target (15%): Close when position gains 15% of premium received.
    $1,439 credit (30 contracts) → ~$203 profit per day when profit target is hit.
  • C5
    Stop Loss (45%): Close a threatened side when cost reaches 45% of credit.
    $1,439 credit → stop at $647 cost on one side. Since 45% < 100% of credit received, even stopped-out days are net positive — no day results in a loss.
